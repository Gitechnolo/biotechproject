# Project Governance - BiotechProject 🧬

## 1. Philosophy: The "Father of the Project" Model
BiotechProject follows a **Benevolent Dictator For Life (BDFL)** governance model, inspired by the leadership structures of foundational open-source projects like the Linux Kernel.

* **Lead Maintainer:** Fabrizio (@Gitechnolo) retains ultimate authority over the project's technical direction, core architecture, and final approval of all Pull Requests.
* **Core Principles:** No contribution or external influence shall compromise the **Zero-Framework Mandate (ADR-001)** or the **Privacy-by-Architecture** (100% client-side data processing).

## 2. Decision Making & ADR Process
Technical evolution is strictly managed through **Architectural Decision Records (ADR)** to ensure SRE-grade reliability.
* Any major change or feature request must be proposed as an Issue for architectural review.
* The Lead Maintainer evaluates proposals based on their impact on the **Global Resilience Score** and technical maturity.
* Decisions are documented as ADRs to ensure long-term stability and transparency, mirroring the rigor of existing records (ADR-001 to ADR-005).

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities
* **Lead Maintainer (The "Father"):** Defines the long-term vision, manages the security of the "Stateless Edge," and acts as the final arbiter for the **Metabolic Digital Twin Architecture**.
* **Maintainers:** Trusted contributors appointed by the Lead Maintainer to oversee specific modules, such as i18n expansion or WHO Data Integration.
* **Contributors:** Community members providing code, scientific content, or translations. Contributions are meritocratic and must pass the **AI-Assisted SRE Auditing (ADR-003)** protocol.

## 4. Financial & Institutional Neutrality
BiotechProject may accept grants or institutional hosting (e.g., from global open-source foundations) to scale its impact, but it maintains strict technical independence.
* **Grant Usage:** External funds are strictly allocated to project milestones, such as **Offline Sync Engine** development, **WCAG AAA** audits, and **Replicability Toolkits**.
* **Autonomy:** Institutional support or financial contributions do not grant decision-making power over the core architecture or the "Zero-Framework" philosophy.

## 5. Open Source Guarantee
BiotechProject is and will always remain open source.
* **Source Code:** Distributed under the **MIT License**.
* **Content & Documentation:** Distributed under **CC BY 4.0**.
* Any attempt to enforce proprietary "lock-in" or vendor dependency is strictly prohibited by this charter.
